Internships

Fill out this application!

The SPREES Internship currently offers Stanford students the opportunity to intern for a local high school or elementary school teacher. It will soon expand to include opportunities for shadowing principals and superintendents as well (with the teacher shadowship serving as a prerequisite), and may eventually expand to include full year-long fellowships.* This program is inspired by a similar internship that affords pre-meds the chance to shadow emergency room physicians.

The SPREES Internship seeks to dispel student myths and misperceptions about the teaching profession. We believe that students need a low-stakes space to discover what teaching truly encompasses before deciding to commit to the classroom after graduation. We also believe that students who want to enter any career in education - whether policy, research, law, tech, administration, or non-profit sectors - must have some understanding of the real challenges that teachers face to make any substantial difference in the system.

This 10-week experience allows interns to target their classroom observation through different lenses. A syllabus might follow this sample trajectory:

  • Week 1: Classroom Spaces & Learning Environments
  • Week 2: Diversity in the Classroom
  • Week 3: Child & Adolescent Development
  • Week 4: Motivating & Supporting Students
  • Week 5: Classroom Management
  • Week 6: Subject-Specific Pedagogy
  • Week 7: Standards & Curriculum
  • Week 8: Formative & Summative Assessment
  • Week 9: Lesson Planning
  • Week 10: The Bigger Picture of Education Reform

Each week, interns begin with a 1-hour workshop led by Stanford faculty, spend 4 hours in the classroom with their mentor teacher, and conclude with a 1-hour reflection and debrief on their observations and experiences. Brief assignments include short readings assigned by the week's workshop leader and 1- to 2-page journal reflections.
